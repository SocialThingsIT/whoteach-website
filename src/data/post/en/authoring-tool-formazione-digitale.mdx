## From PowerPoint to immersive pathways: the evolution of creation tools

Even today, many companies and training institutions are stuck with static content: PDFs, PowerPoint presentations, or linear videos. According to the “Global Corporate Training Market 2024” report by ResearchAndMarkets, over 50% of corporate training content remains non-interactive, with very limited engagement and personalization. This creates not only a flat learning experience but also slows down those who need to acquire operational skills quickly and demonstrate results immediately.

Switching to next-generation authoring tools represents a radical shift: from passive learning to active, trackable, personalized learning experiences. Research by Brandon Hall Group shows that effective e-learning can reduce training time by 40–60% compared to traditional formats, while retention and learning outcomes improve by 25–60%. Additionally, organizations that design interactive, modular content experience a 55% increase in course completion rates and a +30% improvement in skills retention.

Another major advantage is scalability and speed: while traditional methods require weeks, cloud-based tools powered by generative AI can produce an interactive, SCORM-compatible course in under 48 hours. This means responding immediately to organizational or regulatory needs and integrating training directly into operational workflows.

Finally, using these tools is not just an operational decision—it’s strategic. Content stops being a fixed asset and becomes a reusable, updatable, scalable resource, forming the foundation of a culture of continuous learning and modern knowledge management.

## Under the lens: the must-have features

When choosing an authoring tool for corporate training, the key word is “concreteness.” You need something robust, scalable, and data-driven. Here are five features you should never compromise on:

### 1. SCORM (and xAPI): the fast lane to full traceability

It’s not enough to say “the course is good.” You must know who completed it, how well they understood it, and where they struggled. SCORM 1.2, SCORM 2004, and xAPI are the only formats accepted in corporate ecosystems because they enable perfect audits, detailed reporting, and clear ROI measurement.

### 2. Responsive design and mobile learning

Today, 67% of professionals access training via smartphone or tablet. If your tool does not automatically adapt content to all devices, you're losing a significant share of users—and results.

### 3. Integrated Generative AI

This is no longer a “nice-to-have.” Generating slides, scripts, quizzes, and concept maps in minutes allows you to respond quickly to new needs—regulatory, organizational, or corporate. WhoTeach, for example, uses a Generative AI system optimized for corporate training: conversational prompts, module consistency, and personalized output.

### 4. Smart collaboration and versioning

Corporate training means teamwork: multiple authors, feedback cycles, and frequent updates. You need a system that enables real-time collaboration, comments, permission management, and version history. These features are essential to keep the project under control and always up to date.

### 5. Adaptive and intelligent assessments

Multiple-choice quizzes are no longer enough. You need branched scenarios, realistic simulations, and dynamic evaluations that adapt to each participant’s level. This way you can understand what they really learned, where they struggled, and how to intervene effectively.
