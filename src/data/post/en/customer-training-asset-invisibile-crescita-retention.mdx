## What is customer training?

Customer training is a structured, scalable, and measurable learning initiative designed to equip customers with the skills they need to use a product or service effectively, confidently, and productively.

In B2B environments — especially for complex software or subscription-based platforms (SaaS) — customer training is what turns a simple purchase into true customer success. It is the bridge between your value proposition and the customer's ability to translate it into real outcomes.

E-learning delivery makes this process more accessible and efficient through video modules, microlearning, personalized paths, and digital onboarding, all available on-demand with progress tracking and continuous optimization based on real data.

It is not an extra service — it is a strategic asset. Companies that invest in customer training develop more satisfied customers, reduce pressure on support teams, and increase cross-selling and upselling opportunities.

## Why is it important?

Investing in customer training is crucial for several reasons:

- **Increased product adoption and engagement:** customer training programs increase product adoption by an average of 38% and engagement by 31%. Trained customers explore more features and integrate the product into their workflows, maximizing perceived value.
- **Reduced churn and higher retention:** customer education programs boost customer retention by an average of 22%.
- **Lower support costs:** companies investing in customer training experience a 16% decrease in support requests and a 7% reduction in annual support costs.
- **Higher satisfaction and NPS:** customer training increases satisfaction by an average of 26%. Satisfied customers are more likely to recommend the product, generating new business through word of mouth.

Customer training is not only helpful. It is efficient, scalable, and strategically advantageous — similar to corporate e-learning, which we covered in another article. These tools drastically improve usability and engagement, and the numbers confirm it:

- 88% of companies adopting online training solutions saw increased customer retention
- 82% recorded improved customer satisfaction

## How to do customer training effectively

Initial customer training aims to provide key information and skills that have a strong impact.

It can start with free learning content covering onboarding and frequently asked questions: short videos, micro-modules, interactive articles, and quick guides. At this stage, training integrates with customer support: it answers questions, prevents tickets, and turns critical moments into educational opportunities.

But that is only the first step. Once customers complete the onboarding phase, companies can evolve toward multi-level learning paths, with more advanced modules, content personalized by role or industry, and subscription-based or premium learning programs.

After customers acquire basic competencies, customer training can expand to cover a broader range of topics, challenges, and use scenarios.
